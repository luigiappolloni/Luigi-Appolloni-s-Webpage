Scientific Interests

During my Ph.D., I studied the existence of solutions to some semilinear and quasilinear PDEs, involving both local and non-local operators. I used variational and topological methods to study problems where there was a loss of compactness, either due to the unboundedness of the domain or the presence of a critical Sobolev exponent.

More recently, I have become interested in PDEs arising from differential geometry, in particular minimal/CMC surfaces and harmonic maps. In this context, I am working on better understanding the so-called bubbling phenomenon along a sequence of solutions.
